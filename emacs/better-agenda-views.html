Better Agenda Views with Less Lisp

Posted April 12, 2025 by Bill

Let’s be honest: Org Agenda is a brilliant mess. Once you get used to the idea that your entire life is a series of collapsible list items, it’s hard to go back. But then you stare at a screen full of mismatched categories and oversized tag columns and think:

“Why does everything look... off?”

Turns out, the default agenda view isn't optimized for people who like visual clarity. It wastes space. The category column is always the same width — even if your categories are one-word long. And that’s the part that finally broke me.

The Problem

Here’s what a typical tags view might look like:


  ProjectX:         TODO Write report                  :urgent:
  HomeStuff:        TODO Fix sink                      :chores:
  LongCategoryName: TODO Clean inbox                   :admin:

Notice how all the alignment depends on that longest category? If you’ve got a few outliers, your whole layout suffers.

The Fix (Minimal Lisp Required)

I didn’t want to write a full display engine. I just wanted the category column to stop hogging space. After some spelunking, I found this:


(setq org-agenda-prefix-format
      '((agenda . "  %-12:c%?-12t% s")
        (todo . "  %-12:c")
        (tags . "  %-12:c")
        (search . "  %-12:c")))

That %-12:c controls the width of the category column. If you make it smaller — say %-8:c — suddenly your agenda breathes again.

Even better, you can ditch the category column entirely in some views:


(setq org-agenda-prefix-format
      '((tags . "  %?-12t% s")))

Bonus tip: If you use custom agenda commands, you can tailor prefix formatting per view — making your “writing mode” compact and your “project review mode” more verbose.

The Real Win

Org Agenda shouldn’t feel like a log file from 1999. With a few tweaks, it becomes clean, focused, and dare I say — joyful?

I’ve got a bunch more small fixes like this queued up — fewer tweaks, more payoff. And no yak shaving required.
