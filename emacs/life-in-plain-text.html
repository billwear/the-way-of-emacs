How I Run My Life from a Text File

Posted April 12, 2025 by Bill

I manage everything — my job, my house, my support queue, my prayer life, my sci-fi worldbuilding — with a handful of .org files and a system I trust more than any app I’ve ever used. This isn’t a productivity flex. It’s survival.

At the heart of the system is Emacs and org-mode, backed by capture templates, custom TODO states, and one principle: keep friction where it matters.

Agenda-Driven Life

Here’s how my Emacs is set up to focus on the things I care about:


(setq org-agenda-files '("~/etc/org/pdp.org"
                         "~/etc/org/support.org"
                         "~/etc/org/renovation.org"
                         "~/etc/org/neptune.org"))

My agenda view pulls from four key areas: professional goals, community support, house projects, and fiction work. I don’t try to merge them. I just let them show up on the same screen. The overlap reminds me that life is one thing, not four separate apps.

Professional Goals (PDP)

In pdp.org, I keep a running list of expectations tied to my role level — these aren’t aspirations, they’re part of the job description. I treat them as scaffolding, not pressure:


* Professional Objectives                                :pdp:
** TODO Own project documentation
** TODO Improve architecture understanding
** TODO Give product feedback that lands
** TODO Contribute to team goals as a writing expert

Each of these links to real tasks. I log progress underneath them. It keeps me focused on the kind of person I’m becoming, not just the task list du jour.

Support Is Messy — I Track That Too

support.org is a wild mix: actual customer support moments, internal questions, home maintenance, and small acts of neighborly triage. I use a capture template to toss these in quickly:


("s" "Support [Inbox]" entry
 (file+headline "~/etc/org/support.org" "Inbox")
 "* TODO %?\n  %U\n")

Here’s what a real section might look like:


* TODO Pull weeds out front before rain                 :chores:
  <2025-04-14 Mon>
* TODO Answer Jim’s PXE bug question                    :work:
  <2025-04-12 Sat>
* TODO Call neighbor re: loose dog                      :local:
  <2025-04-13 Sun>

This file reminds me that support isn’t just tickets and code — it’s what you choose to carry.

Capture Templates Make This Flow

I use capture templates to instantly drop thoughts, tasks, or logs into the right context without ever switching files manually. A few favorites:


(setq org-capture-templates
      '(("s" "Support [Inbox]" entry
         (file+headline "~/etc/org/support.org" "Inbox")
         "* TODO %?\n  %U\n")
        ("r" "Renovation Task" entry
         (file+headline "~/etc/org/renovation.org" "Projects")
         "* TODO %?  :renovation:\n  %U\n")
        ("l" "Log Entry" entry
         (file+headline "~/etc/org/pdp.org" "Log")
         "* %U %?\n" :empty-lines 1)))

Each one gives me frictionless capture, but keeps tasks filed where they belong.

Friction is a Feature

Most people want less friction. I don’t — not always. For habits I want to keep, I make them repeat. But I don’t use tools that hide or snooze them. I let them accumulate. That way, when I ignore a habit, it piles up like guilt in my agenda view:


* TODO Stretch (repeat every day)                       :habit:
  SCHEDULED: <2025-04-12 Sat +1d>
* TODO Review goals (repeat every Friday)              :habit:
  SCHEDULED: <2025-04-11 Fri +1w>

There’s no “Done” button that resets it. I have to manually mark it. If I ignore it, I see it. That friction is what makes me act. The guilt is the feature.

Everything, Together

All of this flows into a single agenda view that might show me:

  • Professional deliverables
  • Support tasks from people I care about
  • House projects due before the family reunion
  • A prayer list I quietly keep updating

This system isn’t about minimalism. It’s about visible complexity — managed with quiet tools and loud intentions.

Plain text. No distractions. Just the life I’m building, one entry at a time.
