The idea, not the editor

The Way of Emacs

March 4, 2025

Modern life’s a mess — loud, cluttered, and full of junk pulling us in every direction. Screens steal time, apps burn energy, and before you know it, your focus, paycheck, and peace of mind are taking hits.

But underneath all that noise, there’s a quieter, stronger way to live — one that’s flexible, deliberate, and fully yours to shape.

No, this isn’t a pitch to learn Emacs (though it is a beast of a tool). It’s about what Emacs stands for: a mindset. A way to cut through the noise and build your own system — for work, for life, for whatever matters.

Emacs is just the metaphor. Use it or don’t. But if you’re tired of letting default settings call the shots, you might be ready to start designing something better.

The core ideas:

  • Life is modular – Treat your habits, tools, and routines like building blocks. Mix, match, replace.
  • Chase the signal – Ditch the fluff. Clarity, deep work, and purpose beat noise every time.
  • Shape your system – Don’t follow someone else’s map. Learn enough to chart your own.
  • Customize everything – Default settings are for default lives. Tweak what doesn’t fit.
  • Think in plain text – Forget the flashy. Stay simple, readable, and built to last.
  • Digital sufficiency isn’t about using less tech — it’s about using it on your terms. Drop what doesn’t work. Hack what does. Live on purpose.

This is The Way of Emacs: Lead when it’s your turn, follow when it makes sense, but always walk your own path.
