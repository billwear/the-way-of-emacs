The daily standup.

The daily stand-up in Agile is a glorified status huddle disguised as a ritual of empowerment. Its actual purpose is:

📍Purpose

  • Synchronize the team: Who’s doing what, where the overlap is, and who’s about to break the build.
  • Identify blockers early: Not solve them—just throw them on the bonfire so they can be addressed later.
  • Maintain visibility: Everyone sees the same board, and nobody gets to vanish into the void for three days claiming “I was working on it.”
  • Reinforce team cohesion: Brief, ritualized human contact that reminds everyone they are part of a system, not just a weird code hermit.

It is not:

  • A status report for management.
  • A place to explain your life story.
  • A therapy circle. (Tempting, but no.)
